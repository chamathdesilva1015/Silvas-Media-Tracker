DISCOVERY & GAP SCANNER — 5 IMPLEMENTATION STRATEGIES (PC ONLY)
==============================================================
These ideas focus on scanning the internet for movies you *should* have seen (based on cultural impact or personal taste) but haven't added to your tracker yet.

─────────────────────────────────────────────
IDEA 1: ESSENTIAL LIST DIFFING (THE "CULTURE SCORE")
─────────────────────────────────────────────
What it is:
A "Gap Analysis" tool that compares your database against definitive "All-Time Best" lists (IMDb Top 250, Letterboxd Top 250, AFI 100, etc.) to show you what cultural landmarks you are missing.

How it works:
- The backend fetches the latest "Top X" lists from a movie API (TMDB).
- It runs a "diff" against your local database (using fuzzy title matching).
- It presents a "Blindspot Dashboard" showing the top 10 movies from these lists you haven't rated.
- A "Cultural Coverage" stat shows you've seen X% of the "Essential Cinema."

Why it's useful:
It gamifies the "completionist" mindset and ensures you don't miss out on movies that define the medium.

Complexity: Medium (API integration + title matching logic)


─────────────────────────────────────────────
IDEA 2: THE "COMPLETIONIST" SCANNER (DIRECTOR/ACTOR GAPS)
─────────────────────────────────────────────
What it is:
A tool that looks at your favorite directors/actors (people you've rated 9+ multiple times) and finds their other high-rated works you haven't seen.

How it works:
- It identifies "High-Affinity" people in your DB.
- It queries the internet for the rest of their filmography.
- It filters by "Rating > 7.5" and "Not in DB."
- It suggests: "You've seen 4/5 Nolan movies. You're missing 'Insomnia'."

Why it's useful:
If you love a creator's style, you probably *should* have seen their entire catalog. This surfaces the ones that slipped through the cracks.

Complexity: Medium-High (Requires mapping cast/crew for your whole DB)


─────────────────────────────────────────────
IDEA 3: THE "TASTEMAKER" SIMILARITY ENGINE
─────────────────────────────────────────────
What it is:
A discovery tool that uses your own "9s and 10s" to build a taste profile and scans for "Sister Movies" that are highly regarded by the general public.

How it works:
- It takes your top-rated entries and uses a "Similarity API" (like TMDB Recommendations).
- It cross-references the results with your database to remove seen items.
- It further filters by popularity to ensure they are "Big" movies you likely should have heard of.

Why it's useful:
It finds gaps specifically tailored to what *you* like, rather than just generic "Best of" lists.

Complexity: Medium (TMDB Recommendations API is very good for this)


─────────────────────────────────────────────
IDEA 4: "PEAK CINEMA" YEAR-BY-YEAR SCRUBBER
─────────────────────────────────────────────
What it is:
A tool that scans for the highest-rated movies from a specific decade or year that you have a "cluster" in.

How it works:
- If the Stats show you love 90s movies, it scans the top 50 movies of every year in the 90s.
- It flags anything with a high IMDb/TMDB score that isn't in your tracker.
- It displays a "Year in Review" card: "1994 was a big year for you, but you still haven't seen 'Leon: The Professional'."

Why it's useful:
Great for filling in era-specific blindspots when you go on a kick for a certain time period.

Complexity: Low-Medium (Search by year + rating)


─────────────────────────────────────────────
IDEA 5: THE "SEARCH-AHEAD" WATCHLIST BUILDER
─────────────────────────────────────────────
What it is:
A specialized search interface that allows you to search a genre/keyword *on the internet* but highlights what is *not* in your DB as you type.

How it works:
- You type "Cyberpunk" or "Samurai" into a special "Discovery Search" bar.
- It fetches results directly from TMDB/IMDb.
- It styles results that ARE in your DB as "Tracked" (dimmed) and new results as "NEW" (vibrant).
- This lets you quickly scan a genre for things you missed without leaving the site.

Why it's useful:
Manual discovery that stays integrated with your tracking.

Complexity: Low (Live API search with local filter)
